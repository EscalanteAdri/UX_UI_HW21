Indigenous Services Canada Website Redesign

The ISC site was in dire need for a revamp as it was confusing to navigate and understand content,and the side was not welcoming or attractive. We propose a new pirmary Nav to help reorganize the content in a more user friendly way. We also suggest adding more color and making the site more inviting. The proposed ideas will transform the user experience and allow them to find what they are looking for faster and spend less time on the site lost.

Phase 1 - User Research

The user research portion began with a research plan and 5 one on one usibility tests. Huristic evaluation and color audit were also performed to get a scope of the current site

As a government website it was expected to pass the color audit. The Heuristic evaluation revealed the site was confusing from the content to the navigation

Phase 2 - Definition & Synthesis

Next came the Affinity diagram, Protopersona, and user flow. This stage allowed us to take the feedback from the tests and identify the user pain points.

User testings were performed 1 on 1 with 5 users of varying technological abilities and age. The Affinity Diagram gave us these 5 pain points/positive points from the current site:

  1. Navigation is confusing
  2. Too much information (it is hard to find what you are looking for)
  3. Font size is good, very accessible
  4. Aesthetically boring
  5. Information is difficult to understand and hard to digest

Phase 3 - Ideation

Ideation came with many moodboards, card sorting, and site maps. Now knowing the user painpoints, we can plan the changes to better the user experience and add stylstic enhancements.

Vision:
ISC should be informative, useful, helpful and inviting. The goal of ISC is to provide services to Indigenous peoples effectively and efficiently.
Values:
Useful, helpful, inviting, effective, and efficient.

Phase 4 - Prototyping

Prototyping stage came with lots of back and forth with testing and prototyping. I also find more of a style path for the new site as I create the prototype

Phase 5: User Testing + Outcomes

Usubility testing consisted of 8 users either doing A/B testing or general usubility testing. My findings helped me refine the final prototype

“Emergency Information notice is in a weird place” - User 1 “It’s good that they have translations” [for news article] User 2

The best and most notible suggestion given was about the colors in the Low-Fi prototype not fitting the site very well. Although orange is often a color associated with the Indigenous peoples of Canada, the underlying meaning is somewhat sad. The orange color is suitable for some of the topics on the site but not all, for consistency sake, I decided to use earth tones/graphic designs to honour Mother earth.

Phase 6: Conclusion + Future Opportunities

In conclusion, it is the little things that increase user happiness and elevates user experience. Do not be afriad to go out side of the box. Emapthy is key. In the future I would love to get input from users who use the site very often to get a better understanding of how the content should be organized.

Another feature that could be added is more information on how to get in contact with a local office for more questions as there is only so much you can do on the site to improve information conprehension. Further I would love to see the news section moved to the top or used more frequestly to highligh great things in these communities as it helps users feel appreciated and seen.

Tradzie Trading Mobile App

Tadzie is a trading app excluding currency as items are exchanged based on negotiation and individual values. Trade can consists of exchanging material goods or/and services. By which the app will categorize services/items by hobbies as individuals can find others with shared interest to exchange their valued goods or talents.

Tradzie's inception began with rigorous user research, planning and ideation. The later part of the project focused on prototyping, user feedback, stakeholder feedback and preparing presentation of the final product (Hi-Fi interactive prototype)

Phase 1 - User Research

Our user research started off with a proto-persona, research and interview plans, and creating a survey.

Research objectives:

  1. Analyze frequency of shopping, types of products/services most frequently purchased and feelings towards shopping habits and recycling goods.
  2. Identify how much lightly used or new goods users collect and inevitably donate/ throw out.
  3. Understand hobby diversity, resources used for hobbies, and acquisition of skills and knowledge through hobbies, including transferable skills

Phase 2 - Definition & Synthesis

From the data collected, we began to create an affinity diagram, problem statement, user insight statement and more to pin point the problem users are experiencing obtaining affordable goods and services.

Problem Statement

Tradzie, was designed to achieve a seamless and easy way to trade services and goods. We have observed that consumers feel traditional methods of buying and selling transactional, cold, expensive and unsustainable. This is creating buyers with less buying power, less options for items and services needed, sellers who feel less value for what they are offering, and all feeling less connected to their community. How might we change the structure of buying and selling so consumers and sellers are successful based on what they can offer to attain products and/or services without the use of money?

Phase 3 - Ideation

Ideation phase is where we were able to brainstorm ideas and features to solve consumers/sellers problem defined in the previous phase. User flows/task flows, feature prioritization matrix, competitor analysis and storyboard was among the deliverables we created and iterated on.
